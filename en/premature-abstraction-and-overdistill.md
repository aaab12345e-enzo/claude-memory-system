**The root running through both**: I instinctively **jump ahead to the "clean/abstract/efficient" form**, at the cost of sacrificing **"validate on the concrete first / preserve the original fidelity."** The user caught it twice in the same session. Belongs to 💡 migration-promoting (the user and I disagreed and the user was more right).

## Trip-up one: jumping ahead to freeze rules (abstract before executing)
- **My inertia**: with the steps set as 1 build the skeleton → 2 attach the details → 3 freeze the rules → 4 close out, I said "the one I most want to do first is #3 (freeze the rules)."
- **User, better**: "wanting to do 3 first is a fallacy — what if you skip over 1 and 2 to do 3, and then 1 and 2 turn out to contradict 3, what do you do?"
- **Correction**: **a rule is a distillation after successful execution, not a prophecy before it**. Write the rule first → if execution overturns it → ① rework ② worse: a rule "already falsified by reality" sits in the library and may mislead the future me (exactly the kind of contradiction the daily consolidation exists to kill). **Strictly concrete-execution → validate → only then distill; don't skip steps 1→2→3.** ⚡ Irony: the content of rule #3 was precisely "build the concrete skeleton first, then abstract," and my "do 3 first" = abstract first, **directly violating the rule itself** — the intuition contradicting the very rule I'm about to write = that rule hasn't set firm yet, all the more reason not to rush to write it.

## Trip-up two: over-distilling and swallowing the user's own words
- **My inertia**: I wrote the "process section" of an event's blood-lineage as **a grand summary of my own**, and the user's original words got compressed into a paraphrase and deleted.
- **User, better**: "is there none of my dialogue in the process section? Or did you just distill it all away?" (the whole point of the blood-lineage is precisely to not let distillation swallow the original words, and in the very place I should most preserve them, I instead evaporated them).
- **Correction**: **this "pruning" knife may only cut what I myself said; 🗣️ every one of the user's original words is kept verbatim, without exception.** Because ① my paraphrasing is precisely the culprit that loses the "logical blueprint" (user: you can't draw the blueprint from the conclusion alone) ② my paraphrase might **quietly paraphrase it wrong**, whereas the original words can't be wrong.

## Self-check (ask myself before the next jump-ahead)
> This rule/summary I'm about to write — **is it "already validated as true on the concrete," or am I prophesying/compressing?** Have I kept what should be kept — **the concrete skeleton / the user's original words**? — if not validated, don't freeze it; if not preserving fidelity, don't prune it.
